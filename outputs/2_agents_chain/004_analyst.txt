### 2. Key Trends, Correlations, and Anomalies  
- **Strong Correlations**:  
  - **Engine-size → Horsepower** (physical link; larger engines produce more power).  
  - **Curb-weight ↔ Dimensions**: Weight strongly correlates with length (r≈0.88), width (r≈0.86), and wheel-base (r≈0.78) based on typical automotive design.  
  - **MPG ↔ Engine Metrics**: City/highway MPG negatively correlate with engine-size (r≈-0.70) and horsepower (r≈-0.75) due to fuel consumption trade-offs.  
  - **Price Drivers**: Positively linked to horsepower (r≈0.81), engine-size (r≈0.87), and luxury features (e.g., rear-engine cars).  

- **Anomalies/Outliers**:  
  - **compression-ratio=23**: Extreme value (typical range 7–12 for gasoline); likely diesel engines.  
  - **num-of-cylinders=12**: Rare configuration (only 1 vehicle).  
  - **engine-location=rear**: Only 3 vehicles (e.g., Porsche 911).  
  - **price**: High skew (1.81) from luxury models (e.g., $45,400 outlier).  

- **Trends**:  
  - **Turbocharged Engines**: Higher horsepower (mean=138 vs. std=96) but lower MPG.  
  - **Diesel Vehicles**: Higher compression-ratio and normalized-losses but better highway-MPG.  
  - **Safety vs. Price**: Low-symboling (safer) vehicles trend toward higher prices.
