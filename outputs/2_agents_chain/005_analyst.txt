### 2. Key Trends, Correlations, and Anomalies  
- **Strong Correlations**:  
  - **Size ↔ Weight**: `wheel-base`, `length`, `width`, and `curb-weight` correlate positively (e.g., larger dimensions = higher weight).  
  - **Engine ↔ Performance**: `engine-size`, `horsepower`, and `num-of-cylinders` correlate (e.g., 12-cylinder engines have max `engine-size`=326).  
  - **Efficiency ↔ Performance**: `city-mpg`/`highway-mpg` negatively correlate with `engine-size` (r ≈ -0.7) and `horsepower` (r ≈ -0.8).  
  - **Price Drivers**: `price` correlates with `engine-size` (r ≈ 0.9), `horsepower` (r ≈ 0.8), and luxury features (e.g., RWD, turbo).  

- **Anomalies**:  
  - **compression-ratio=23**: Physically implausible for standard engines; likely diesel (diesel avg: 15–23) or data error.  
  - **Rare Values**: Rear-engine cars (3), twelve-cylinder engines (outliers in `num-of-cylinders`), and turbocharged engines (only 37).  
  - **Missing Data**: `normalized-losses` (20% missing) and `price` (4 missing) limit analysis.  

- **Trends**:  
  - Most cars are FWD, gas-powered sedans/hatchbacks with 4-cylinder OHC engines.  
  - High-performance cars (e.g., turbo, RWD, >6 cylinders) command premium prices.
