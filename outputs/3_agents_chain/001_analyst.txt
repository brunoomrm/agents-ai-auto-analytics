### 1. Variable Analysis (Individual Breakdown)

   - **Meaning**: Insurance risk rating (-3 = low risk, 3 = high risk).

   - **Anomalies**: None (no missing values).  

2. **normalized-losses (float64)**:  
   - **Meaning**: Average loss payments per insured vehicle year (relative scale).  
   - **Units**: Arbitrary normalized units (65–256).  
   - **Distribution**: Right-skewed (skew=0.77), mean=122. High missing rate (20%).  

3. **make (object)**:  
   - **Meaning**: Vehicle manufacturer.

### 2. Key Trends, Correlations, and Anomalies  
- **Strong Correlations**:  
  - **Engine metrics (engine-size, horsepower, cylinders)** correlate positively with **price** (luxury/high-performance vehicles).  
  - **Physical dimensions (length, width, curb-weight)** correlate with **wheel-base** (larger vehicles have longer wheelbases).  
  - **Fuel efficiency (city-mpg, highway-mpg)** negatively correlates with **horsepower** and **engine-size** (powerful engines consume more fuel).  
  - **Turbocharged aspiration** links to higher **compression-ratio** (e.g., max=23) and **horsepower**.  

- **Anomalies/Outliers**:  
  - **Rear-engine vehicles** (3 records) are rare and may skew drivetrain analysis.  
  - **High-risk vehicles (symboling=3)** often have turbocharged engines and high horsepower.  
  - **Diesel engines** show extreme **compression-ratio** (up to 23 vs. gasoline avg=9).  
  - **Price outliers** ($45,400) align with max **engine-size** (326) and **horsepower** (288).  

- **Missing Data Hotspots**:  
  - **normalized-losses** (20% missing) limits risk analysis.  
  - **bore/stroke** (4 missing) and **horsepower/peak-rpm** (2 missing) affect engine performance insights.

### 3. Enrichment Recommendations  
1. **Safety Ratings (NHTSA/IIHS)**:  
   - Map to **symboling** and **price** to quantify risk-price relationships.  
   - Source: NHTSA crash test datasets (join by **make** and **body-style**).  

2. **Emissions Data (EPA)**:  
   - Add CO₂ emissions tied to **fuel-type** and **engine-size**.  
   - Enhances analysis of **city-mpg/highway-mpg** environmental impact.  

3. **Vehicle Class Segmentation**:  
   - Add "vehicle-class" (e.g., SUV, sedan, sports) using **body-style** and **curb-weight**.  
   - Source: EPA vehicle classification standards.  

4. **Market Data (Edmunds/KBB)**:  
   - Include resale value, model year, or sales volume to contextualize **price** outliers.  
   - Join by **make** and **engine-type**.  

5. **Technical Specifications**:  
   - Add transmission type (manual/automatic) to explain **peak-rpm** variance.  
   - Include torque values to complement **horsepower**.  

6. **Geographical Attributes**:  
   - Add "country-of-origin" for **make** (e.g., Germany for BMW) to explore regional trends.  

**Targeted Enrichment for Existing Gaps**:  
- **normalized-losses**: Merge with IIHS insurance claim data by **make** and **body-style**.  
- **num-of-cylinders**: Add cylinder configuration (V6, inline-4) for granular engine analysis.  
- **diesel vehicles**: Enrich with urea/AdBlue usage flags to explain high **compression-ratio**.
