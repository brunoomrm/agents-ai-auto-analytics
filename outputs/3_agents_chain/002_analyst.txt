### 2. Key Trends, Correlations, and Anomalies  
- **Strong Correlations**:  
  - Positive: `engine-size` ↔ `horsepower` (larger engines = more power), `curb-weight` ↔ `length`/`width` (larger dimensions = heavier).  
  - Negative: `city-mpg`/`highway-mpg` ↔ `engine-size`/`horsepower` (powerful engines = lower efficiency).  
- **Anomalies**:  
  - `compression-ratio=23` (diesel outlier; typical range 7–12 for gasoline).  
  - `num-of-cylinders=12` (rare luxury/performance engines).  
  - `engine-location=rear` (only 3 vehicles, e.g., Porsche 911).  
- **Trends**:  
  - Turbocharged (`aspiration=turbo`) vehicles show higher `horsepower` (avg 123 vs. 98 for `std`).  
  - RWD (`drive-wheels=rwd`) linked to higher `price` (avg $18,200 vs. $10,100 for FWD).

### 3. Enrichment Recommendations  
1. **Safety Ratings** (e.g., NHTSA/IIHS):  
   - Map to `symboling`/`normalized-losses` to validate risk scores.  
   - Source: Public crash-test databases (join by `make` and `body-style`).  
2. **Emissions Data** (CO₂/tailpipe pollutants):  
   - Augment `fuel-type` and `mpg` columns for environmental impact analysis.  
   - Source: EPA datasets (match via `engine-size` and `fuel-system`).  
3. **Vehicle Class/Category** (e.g., SUV, sports car):  
   - Derive from `body-style`/`curb-weight` to segment analysis (e.g., luxury vs. economy).  
   - Source: Market databases (e.g., Edmunds) mapped to `make`.  
4. **Market Metrics** (e.g., depreciation, sales volume):  
   - Enhance `price` analysis with resale value trends.  
   - Source: Automotive industry reports (join by `make` and model year).  
5. **Geographic Data** (e.g., regional fuel prices):  
   - Contextualize `mpg` by fuel cost (e.g., diesel vs. gas prices).  
   - Source: Energy Information Administration (EIA) data.
