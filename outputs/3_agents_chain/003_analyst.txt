### Key Trends, Correlations, and Anomalies  
- **Strong Correlations**:  
  - **Size ↔ Weight**: `length`, `width`, `wheel-base` correlate positively with `curb-weight` (larger vehicles are heavier).  
  - **Performance ↔ Efficiency**: `horsepower` and `engine-size` negatively correlate with `city-mpg`/`highway-mpg` (powerful engines reduce fuel economy).  
  - **Drive Wheels ↔ Price**: `drive-wheels=rwd` (rear-wheel drive) likely links to higher `price` (sports/luxury vehicles).  
- **Anomalies**:  
  - **Compression Ratio**: Value=23 (vs. mean=10.1) indicates diesel engines—verify with `fuel-type=diesel`.  
  - **Engine Location**: 3 `rear`-engine vehicles (e.g., Porsche 911) are outliers vs. 202 `front`-engine.  
  - **Cylinders**: 12-cylinder engines (max `num-of-cylinders`) skew distribution (skew=2.82).  
- **Missing Data**: `normalized-losses` (20% missing) needs imputation. `bore`/`stroke`/`price` have 1–2% missing.  
- **Unexpected Trend**: `symboling` (risk) shows no clear linear relationship with `price`—luxury cars may have mixed risk scores.

### Enrichment Recommendations  
1. **Safety Ratings** (e.g., NHTSA/IIHS crash tests):  
   - Map to `make` and `body-style` to explain `symboling` and `normalized-losses`.  
2. **CO2 Emissions Data** (EPA databases):  
   - Augment `city-mpg`/`highway-mpg` with environmental impact metrics.  
3. **Vehicle Class** (e.g., SUV, sedan, sports):  
   - Derive from `body-style`/`curb-weight` to segment analysis (e.g., "SUVs have lower mpg").  
4. **Market Data** (sales volume, model year):  
   - Source from automotive reports; join via `make` to contextualize `price` outliers.  
5. **Transmission Type** (manual/automatic):  
   - Add column to refine `horsepower`/`peak-rpm` analysis (e.g., automatics may have higher city-mpg).  
6. **Torque Specifications**:  
   - Supplement `horsepower` for performance analysis (e.g., high torque improves towing capacity).  
7. **Geographic Preferences** (regional sales data):  
   - Explain `fuel-type` bias (e.g., diesel popularity in Europe) via `make` origins.  

**Actionable Insight**: Prioritize merging safety ratings and CO2 data to enhance risk/efficiency analysis. Model year would resolve cohort effects (e.g., older cars in `normalized-losses`).
