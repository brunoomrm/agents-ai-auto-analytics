### 2. Key Trends, Correlations, and Anomalies

- **Strong Correlations**:  
  - **Size/Weight ↔ Performance**: `wheel-base`, `length`, `width`, `curb-weight` correlate positively with `engine-size` (e.g., larger vehicles = heavier, larger engines).  
  - **Performance ↔ Efficiency**: `engine-size` and `horsepower` negatively correlate with `city-mpg`/`highway-mpg` (R² likely >0.7).  
  - **Aspiration ↔ Power**: `turbo` engines show higher `horsepower` (sample: turbo models avg ~140 HP vs. std ~95 HP).  
  - **Drive Wheels ↔ Body Style**: `rwd` common in convertibles (sample), `fwd` in sedans/hatchbacks.  

- **Anomalies**:  
  - **compression-ratio=23**: Extreme value; likely diesel engines (verify with `fuel-type=diesel`).  
  - **engine-size=326**: Outlier (e.g., high-end sports cars).  
  - **horsepower=288**: Matches luxury/performance brands (e.g., Porsche).  
  - **rear engine-location**: Only 3 vehicles (e.g., Porsche 911).  

- **Trends**:  
  - Diesel engines (`fuel-type=diesel`) have higher `compression-ratio` (avg ~20 vs. gas ~9).  
  - `symboling` risk increases with `horsepower` (positive correlation).

1. **Safety Ratings** (e.g., NHTSA/IIHS):  
   - Map to `make`/`body-style` to explain `symboling` and `normalized-losses`.  
   - Source: [NHTSA Vehicle API](https://www.nhtsa.gov/).  

2. **Emissions Data** (CO₂/tailpipe pollutants):  
   - Augment `fuel-type`/`engine-size` to analyze environmental impact.  
   - Source: EPA Fuel Economy Dataset.  

3. **Vehicle Class** (e.g., SUV, sedan, sports):  
   - Derive from `curb-weight`/`length` or add externally (e.g., Edmunds API) to segment analysis.  

4. **Market/Historical Data**:  
   - Add `model-year` (missing) to track trends. Source: Automotive news archives.  
   - Include `sales-volume` to identify popular models.  

5. **Technical Specifications**:  
   - Add `torque` (correlates with `horsepower`) and `transmission-type` (affects `mpg`).  
   - Source: Manufacturer databases.  

6. **Geographic Origin**:  
   - Add `country-of-origin` (e.g., Germany for BMW) to `make` for cultural/regulatory insights.
