### 1. Variable Analysis (Individual Breakdown)

   - **Meaning**: Insurance risk rating (-3 = lowest risk, 3 = highest risk). Reflects vehicle safety and claim likelihood.  
   - **Distribution**: Symmetric (skew=0.21), mode=0, range=-3 to 3. Most vehicles cluster near neutral risk (mean=0.83).  

2. **normalized-losses** (float64)  
   - **Meaning**: Average loss payment per insured vehicle year (normalized). Indicates repair cost severity.  
   - **Units**: Monetary units (e.g., USD equivalent).  
   - **Distribution**: 20% missing. Right-skewed (skew=0.77), mean=122, range=65–256. Higher values indicate costlier repairs.  

3. **make** (object)  
   - **Meaning**: Vehicle manufacturer (e.g., Toyota, BMW).  
   - **Categories**: 22 brands. Toyota most frequent (32/205), Nissan second (18/205).  

4. **fuel-type** (object)

5. **aspiration** (object)

6. **num-of-doors** (Int64)

### 2. Key Trends, Correlations, and Anomalies

- **Strong Correlations**:  
  - **Dimensions & Weight**: `length`, `width`, `wheel-base`, and `curb-weight` correlate positively (e.g., larger vehicles weigh more).  
  - **Performance Triad**: `engine-size`, `horsepower`, and `num-of-cylinders` align (e.g., 12-cylinder engines → high HP).  
  - **Efficiency Trade-offs**: `city-mpg`/`highway-mpg` negatively correlate with `engine-size`, `horsepower`, and `curb-weight` (e.g., heavy turbocharged vehicles have lower MPG).  
  - **Price Drivers**: `price` correlates with `engine-size` (r≈0.85 expected), `horsepower`, and luxury features (e.g., RWD/rear-engine).  

- **Anomalies**:  
  - **compression-ratio=23**: Extreme value likely indicating diesel engines (verify with `fuel-type`).  
  - **rear-engine vehicles (3)**: Rare (e.g., Porsche) with high `price` and `horsepower`.  
  - **12-cylinder engines**: High `engine-size` (min=326) and `price` outliers.  
  - **Symboling-risk mismatch**: Some high-performance cars (e.g., turbo) have low-risk ratings (-2/-3), warranting investigation.  

- **Trends**:  
  - Turbocharged (`aspiration=turbo`) vehicles show higher `horsepower` (mean≈140 vs. std≈95) but lower MPG.  
  - Diesel (`fuel-type=diesel`) links to high `compression-ratio` (>15) and lower `peak-rpm`.  
  - RWD/4WD vehicles have higher `curb-weight` and `price` than FWD.

### 3. Enrichment Recommendations

1. **Safety Ratings (NHTSA/IIHS)**  
   - Map to `symboling` and `make` to validate risk scores.  
   - Add: `safety_rating`, `crash_test_score`.  

2. **Environmental Impact**  
   - Augment `fuel-type` and MPG with emissions data.  
   - Add: `co2_emissions`, `fuel_consumption_l/100km`.  

3. **Vehicle Classification**  
   - Derive `vehicle_class` (e.g., SUV, compact) from `body-style` and dimensions. Use EPA standards.  

4. **Market & Temporal Data**  
   - Add `model_year` (missing) to analyze trends. Source: Manufacturer databases.  
   - Include `depreciation_rate` and `sales_volume` by `make`.  

5. **Technical Specifications**  
   - Enhance `engine-type` with `torque` (lb-ft) and `transmission_type` (automatic/manual).  
   - Add `electric_range` for hybrids (though absent in current `fuel-type`).  

6. **Geographic Data**  
   - Map `make` to `country_of_origin` (e.g., Toyota → Japan) for regional analysis.  

7. **Economic Metrics**  
   - Adjust `price` for inflation to `inflation_adjusted_price`.  
   - Add `maintenance_cost` linked to `normalized-losses`.  

**Targeted Enrichment by Variable**:  
- `symboling`: Safety test results (IIHS).  
- `normalized-losses`: Insurance claim frequency (ISO data).  
- `make`: Brand reliability scores (Consumer Reports).  
- `engine-size`: Displacement in liters (standardize units).  
- `price`: Historical MSRP and resale value (Kelley Blue Book).
