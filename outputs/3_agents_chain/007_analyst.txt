### 2. Key Trends, Correlations, and Anomalies

- **Strong Correlations**:  
  - **Dimensions & Weight**: `wheel-base`, `length`, `width`, and `curb-weight` correlate positively (e.g., longer/wider vehicles are heavier).  
  - **Performance Metrics**: `engine-size`, `horsepower`, and `price` show positive correlation (e.g., large engines → high power → high cost).  
  - **Efficiency Trade-offs**: `horsepower` negatively correlates with `city-mpg`/`highway-mpg` (high power → lower fuel efficiency).  
  - **Engine Attributes**: `compression-ratio` >20 links to `fuel-type=diesel` (expected diesel trait).  

- **Anomalies/Outliers**:  
  - **Rear Engines**: Only 3 vehicles (`engine-location=rear`), likely sports cars (e.g., Porsche).  
  - **High Compression**: `compression-ratio=23` (diesel outlier).  
  - **Extreme Values**: Max `horsepower=288` (performance outliers), max `engine-size=326` (large-displacement engines).  
  - **Missing Data**: `normalized-losses` (20% missing), `price` (4 missing), and engine specs (`bore`, `stroke`).  

- **Notable Trends**:  
  - **Risk vs. Safety**: Low `symboling` (safer) vehicles often have higher `curb-weight` (e.g., sedans).  
  - **Turbo Impact**: `aspiration=turbo` associates with higher `horsepower` but lower MPG.  
  - **Diesel Efficiency**: Diesel vehicles show higher `compression-ratio` and better `highway-mpg`.

### 3. Enrichment Recommendations

1. **Safety Ratings (e.g., IIHS/NHTSA)**:  
   - **Map to**: `make` and inferred vehicle class.  
   - **Why**: Explain `symboling` variance and link design to real-world safety.  

2. **Emissions Data (e.g., EPA)**:  
   - **Map to**: `engine-size`, `fuel-type`, `fuel-system`.  
   - **Why**: Augment MPG with CO₂ metrics for environmental impact analysis.  

3. **Vehicle Class/Category (e.g., "SUV", "Sports Car")**:  
   - **Derive from**: `body-style`, `curb-weight`, `wheel-base`.  
   - **Why**: Group vehicles for segment-based trends (e.g., SUV safety vs. sedan efficiency).  

4. **Market/Historical Data**:  
   - **Add**: Model year, sales volume, country of origin.  
   - **Why**: Contextualize `price` and demand (e.g., luxury German vs. economy Japanese).  

5. **Technical Specifications**:  
   - **Add**: Torque, 0-60 mph time, towing capacity.  
   - **Why**: Deepen performance analysis beyond `horsepower` and `engine-size`.  

6. **Geographic Data**:  
   - **Add**: Regional sales data (e.g., via VIN decoders).  
   - **Why**: Identify location-based preferences (e.g., diesel popularity in Europe).  

**Priority Variables for Enrichment**:  
- `symboling` (safety data),  
- `normalized-losses` (insurance databases),  
- `price` (market trends),  
- `fuel-type` (emissions standards).
