### 2. Key Trends, Correlations, and Anomalies  
- **Physical Dimensions**:  
  - Strong positive correlations expected between `wheel-base`, `length`, `width`, `height`, and `curb-weight` (e.g., larger vehicles are heavier).  
  - `curb-weight` and `engine-size` likely correlate positively (heavy vehicles need larger engines).  

- **Performance and Efficiency**:  
  - Negative correlation: `horsepower` vs. `city-mpg`/`highway-mpg` (high power reduces efficiency).  
  - Positive correlation: `compression-ratio` and `fuel-type` (diesel engines have higher ratios, per outlier at 23).  
  - Anomaly: Diesel vehicles (20 entries) may cluster in high `compression-ratio` and low `horsepower`.  

- **Risk and Pricing**:  
  - `symboling` (risk) may correlate with `price` (luxury/sports cars = higher risk) or `horsepower`.  
  - `price` outliers (e.g., $45,400) likely link to high `engine-size`, turbo `aspiration`, or RWD/`rear` `engine-location`.  

- **Anomalies**:  
  - Only 3 rear-engine vehicles (e.g., Porsche 911).  
  - `num-of-cylinders`=12 (extremely rare) and `compression-ratio`=23 (diesel outlier).  
  - Missing values in `normalized-losses` (20%) require investigation.
