- **Size-Weight-Power Correlation**:  
  `wheel-base`, `length`, `width`, `curb-weight`, `engine-size`, and `horsepower` show positive correlations (e.g., large vehicles like SUVs have higher weight, larger engines, and more horsepower). Outliers (e.g., `engine-size`=326, `curb-weight`=4066) likely represent heavy-duty/luxury vehicles.  

- **Efficiency Trade-offs**:  
  `city-mpg` and `highway-mpg` negatively correlate with performance metrics (`horsepower`, `engine-size`). High-MPG outliers (e.g., `city-mpg`=49) suggest compact/efficient models.  

- **Risk and Design**:  
  `symboling` (risk) may correlate with `body-style` (sports cars = higher risk) and `price` (luxury vehicles = higher repair costs).  

- **Anomalies**:  
  - **Compression Ratio=23**: Extreme outlier; typical range is 7–12. May indicate diesel engines (high compression) or data error.  
  - **Horsepower=288**: Performance outlier (e.g., sports cars).
