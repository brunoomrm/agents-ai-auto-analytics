### Key Trends, Correlations, and Anomalies  
- **Strong Correlations**:  
  - **Physical Dimensions**: `wheel-base`, `length`, `width`, and `curb-weight` are tightly linked (e.g., longer/wider vehicles are heavier).  
  - **Engine-Performance**: `engine-size`, `horsepower`, and `num-of-cylinders` correlate positively (e.g., larger engines = more cylinders = higher HP).  
  - **Efficiency Trade-offs**: `city-mpg` and `highway-mpg` strongly correlate (ρ likely >0.9). Both negatively correlate with `engine-size`, `curb-weight`, and `horsepower` (e.g., high HP reduces MPG).  
  - **Price Drivers**: `price` correlates with `engine-size` (r≈0.85 expected), `horsepower`, and luxury features (e.g., rear `engine-location`).  

- **Anomalies/Outliers**:  
  - **compression-ratio**: Value of 23 is extreme (typical max: 12 for gasoline, 20 for diesel). Verify if linked to `fuel-type` (diesel) or data error.  
  - **num-of-cylinders**: 12-cylinder engines (max) are rare outliers; may skew `engine-size` and `price`.  
  - **horsepower**: Max 288 HP is atypical (sports cars); right tail affects skew (1.39).  
  - **symboling**: Negative values (-3 to -1) imply "safer" vehicles; investigate links to `safety` or `body-style`.  

- **Trends**:  
  - **Turbocharging**: `aspiration=turbo` likely associates with higher `horsepower` but lower MPG.  
  - **Diesel Engines**: Rare (`fuel-type=diesel`: 20/205) but may explain high `compression-ratio` and better MPG.  
  - **Weight Efficiency**: Lighter vehicles (`curb-weight` <2000 lbs) achieve highest MPG (e.g., 49 city/54 highway).
