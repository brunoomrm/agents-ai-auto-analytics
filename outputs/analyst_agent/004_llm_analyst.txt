### 2. Key Trends, Correlations, and Anomalies  

- **Strong Correlations**:  
  - **Dimensions → Weight/Engine**: `length`, `width`, `wheel-base` correlate positively with `curb-weight` (larger vehicles are heavier) and `engine-size` (larger engines in larger vehicles).  
  - **Performance ↔ Efficiency**: `horsepower` and `engine-size` negatively correlate with `city-mpg`/`highway-mpg` (high-power engines reduce fuel efficiency).  
  - **Price Drivers**: `price` correlates with `engine-size` (r≈0.9 expected), `horsepower`, and luxury `make` (e.g., Porsche). `symboling` (risk) may inversely correlate with price (safer cars cost more).  

- **Anomalies/Outliers**:  
  - **Engine Extremes**: `engine-size` max=326 (vs. mean=127) and `num-of-cylinders` max=12 indicate high-performance outliers (e.g., sports cars).  
  - **Compression Ratio**: Max=23 (vs. mean=10.1) suggests diesel engines (typical range 15–23), but sparse diesel representation (`fuel-type`: diesel=20/205).  
  - **Price Skew**: 75th percentile=$16,500 vs. max=$45,400 (e.g., luxury brands like Jaguar).  
  - **Rare Features**: `engine-location=rear` (3/205) and `num-of-doors=two` (infrequent vs. four-door dominance).  

- **Trends**:  
  - **Efficiency Trade-offs**: Turbo (`aspiration=turbo`) increases `horsepower` but reduces `city-mpg`.  
  - **Drive Wheels**: `fwd` vehicles dominate, likely due to fuel efficiency focus.  
  - **Risk vs. Body Style**: `symboling` may link to `body-style` (e.g., convertibles=-3 risk, sedans=0).
