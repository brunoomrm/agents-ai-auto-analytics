### 2. Key Trends, Correlations, and Anomalies

- **Strong Correlations**:  
  - **horsepower** ↔ **price** (+): High HP vehicles command premium prices (e.g., 288 HP at $45k).  
  - **engine-size** ↔ **curb-weight** (+): Larger engines increase weight (mean 126.9 ci → 2555 lbs).  
  - **city-mpg** ↔ **highway-mpg** (+): Strong alignment (both right-skewed; efficiency trends consistent).  
  - **width** ↔ **price** (+): Wider vehicles (max 72.3 in) correlate with luxury/performance pricing.  
  - **compression-ratio** ↔ **fuel-type**: Diesel vehicles (high compression, max 23) vs. gas (mean ~9).  

- **Anomalies/Outliers**:  
  - **normalized-losses**: 20% missing + max 256 (far > 75th percentile 150). Suggests data quality issues or extreme-risk vehicles.  
  - **engine-location**: Only 3 rear-engine vehicles (potential data imbalance).  
  - **num-of-cylinders**: Twelve-cylinder engines (extreme rarity, skew=2.82) driving **price** skew.  
  - **compression-ratio**: Max 23 (diesel outlier) vs. mean 10.14.  

- **Efficiency Trends**:  
  - **diesel** vehicles show high **compression-ratio** but lower **horsepower** → trade-off for fuel economy.  
  - **turbo** aspiration likely boosts **horsepower** but reduces **city-mpg** (e.g., turbo vehicles average lower MPG).
