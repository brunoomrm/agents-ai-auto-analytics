### 2. Key Trends, Correlations, and Anomalies

- **Strong Correlations**:  
  - **Size ↔ Weight/Performance**: `wheel-base`, `length`, `width` correlate with `curb-weight` (larger vehicles heavier). `curb-weight` and `engine-size` likely correlate (r ≈ +0.8 expected).  
  - **Performance ↔ Efficiency**: `horsepower` negatively correlates with `city-mpg`/`highway-mpg` (powerful engines less efficient).  
  - **Price Drivers**: `engine-size`, `horsepower`, and luxury brands (e.g., Porsche) drive `price` (e.g., max $45,400).  
  - **Diesel Engines**: High `compression-ratio` (max=23) links to `fuel-type=diesel` (efficiency trait).  

- **Anomalies/Outliers**:  
  - **Performance Extremes**: `horsepower=288` (e.g., sports cars) and `engine-size=326` (large displacement) deviate sharply from means.  
  - **Rare Configurations**: Only 3 `engine-location=rear` (e.g., Porsche 911), 12-cylinder engines.  
  - **Skewed Distributions**: `compression-ratio` (skew=2.61) and `price` (skew=1.81) have long right tails.  

- **Trends**:  
  - **Dominant Designs**: FWD (120), sedans (96), gas engines (185), OHC engines (148).  
  - **Efficiency Trade-offs**: Turbo (`aspiration=turbo`) may boost `horsepower` but reduce MPG.
