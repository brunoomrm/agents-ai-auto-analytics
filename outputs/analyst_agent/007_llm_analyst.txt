### 2. Key Trends, Correlations, and Anomalies

- **Physical Dimensions Correlation**:  
  `wheel-base`, `length`, `width`, and `curb-weight` are strongly correlated (e.g., longer wheelbase → heavier curb-weight). Positive skew in all suggests larger vehicles (SUVs/trucks) are outliers.

- **Engine-Performance Links**:  
  - `engine-size`, `horsepower`, and `num-of-cylinders` correlate positively (e.g., 12-cylinder engines → high horsepower).  
  - `compression-ratio` outlier (23) aligns with diesel `fuel-type` (high compression typical for diesel).  
  - Negative correlation: `engine-size`/`horsepower` vs. `city-mpg`/`highway-mpg` (larger engines → lower fuel efficiency).

- **Price Drivers**:  
  Luxury brands (e.g., Jaguar, Porsche), turbo `aspiration`, rear `engine-location`, and high `horsepower` correlate with high `price`.  
  **Anomaly**: Two identical Alfa Romeo samples (rows 0–1) have different prices ($13.5K vs $16.5K), suggesting non-engine factors (e.g., trim, year).

- **Risk and Losses**:  
  High `symboling` (risk) links to high `normalized-losses` (e.g., sports cars). Missing 20% of `normalized-losses` data may bias risk analysis.

- **Skewness & Outliers**:  
  - Right-skewed: `engine-size` (max=326), `horsepower` (max=288), `price` (max=$45,400).  
  - `compression-ratio` skew (2.61) from diesel engines.  
  - Rare values: rear `engine-location` (3 rows), 12 `num-of-cylinders`.

### 3. Enrichment Recommendations

1. **Safety Ratings (e.g., IIHS/NHTSA)**:  
   - Map to `make`, `body-style`, and `curb-weight` to explain `symboling` (risk).  
   - Source: NHTSA crash test databases.

2. **Emissions Data (CO2/tailpipe pollutants)**:  
   - Augment `fuel-type` and `mpg` variables.  
   - Source: EPA emissions datasets (joinable via `engine-size`/`fuel-system`).

3. **Vehicle Class Segmentation**:  
   - Add "vehicle class" (e.g., compact, SUV) using `length`/`curb-weight` thresholds.  
   - Source: EPA vehicle size classifications.

4. **Market/Historical Context**:  
   - Add "model year" to contextualize `price` depreciation and `mpg` trends.  
   - Source: Automotive industry reports (e.g., Kelley Blue Book).  
   - Add "country of origin" for `make` (e.g., BMW → Germany) for import/export insights.

5. **Feature-Specific Enrichment**:  
   - For `normalized-losses`: Insurance industry datasets (e.g., HLDI) to fill missing 41 values.  
   - For diesel outliers: Add "torque" data (strong in diesel engines) to complement `horsepower`.  
   - For `price` outliers: Luxury/trim features (e.g., leather seats, tech packages).

**Enrichment Strategy**: Join external data via `make` and `body-style`. Use VIN decoders for granular matching.
