### 2. Key Trends, Correlations, and Anomalies  

- **Strong Correlations**:  
  - **Size ↔ Weight**: `wheel-base`, `length`, `width`, and `curb-weight` correlate positively (e.g., larger dimensions = higher weight).  
  - **Power ↔ Efficiency**: `engine-size` and `horsepower` correlate positively (R² likely >0.8), but both negatively correlate with `city-mpg`/`highway-mpg` (e.g., high-power engines reduce MPG).  
  - **Price Drivers**: `engine-size`, `horsepower`, and luxury `make` (e.g., Porsche) strongly correlate with `price`.  
  - **Risk Factors**: High `symboling` (risk) links to RWD (`drive-wheels`), turbo (`aspiration`), and high `horsepower`.  

- **Anomalies/Outliers**:  
  - **Diesel Outliers**: Extreme `compression-ratio` (max=23) likely from diesel engines (verify with `fuel-type`).  
  - **Performance Vehicles**: 12-cylinder engines (`num-of-cylinders`), 326 cu in `engine-size`, and 288 HP (`horsepower`) are rare high-performance outliers.  
  - **Rear-Engine Cars**: Only 3 vehicles with `engine-location=rear` (exotic models).  
  - **Price Skew**: 75th percentile at $16,500 vs. max $45,400 – luxury vehicles distort distribution.  

- **Missing Data Clusters**:  
  - `normalized-losses` (20% missing) likely lacks data for niche models.  
  - `bore`/`stroke`/`price` missing in 2–4 rows – possibly discontinued models.
